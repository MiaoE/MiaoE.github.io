Engineering Science at the University of Toronto is a honours program in the Faculty of Applied Sciencee and Engineering. Some useful courses I've taken are as follow:
  • CIV102: Structures and Materials - This course teaches the foundations of bridges, mainly the tensile and compressive propertiees of different materials, including steel and concrete.
  • ESC180: Introduction to Computer Programming - This course teaches about the basics of programming in python, and some general programming concepts such as lists, sorting algorithms, and idk
  • ESC190: Computer Algorithms and Data Structures - This course is an introductory course to programming in C with concepts learned in python, different types of data structures, and also teaches advanced algorithms in trees, graphs, and hash tables
  • ECE159: Fundamentals of Electric Circuits - This course is an introductory course to electrical engineering. It teaches the basics of properties of electricity and circuit components, and also mathematical calculations involving first-order and second-order circuits.
